About Me

Hi! I'm Andrew Thomas, a full-stack developer with a passion for problem solving and creative thinking. I am currently a senior studying computer science at the College of Staten Island as part of the Macaulay Honors College. I am skilled in a number of programming languages and technologies including C++, C, Java, Javascript, Python, React, Node, Git, Github, SQL, HTML, and CSS. I have used these skills to develop several projects including a drawing application, an application for musicians to learn harmony, and a full-stack application that allows readers to simplify or translate texts in pdfs for better readability. In addition, I am also involved in research focused on machine learning. Currently, I am using models to predict house pricing in New York. I also am the president of the CSI Computer Science Club, where I host workshops and knowledge-based games on computer science. I began my professional career tutoring college students in computer science and math, then went on to a software engineering internship at a startup called Unadat, before my most recent internship with Walmart Global Tech where I built an API and created a page implementing its functionality for the accounts receivable team.
